Best practices for indexing an un-indexed location

Get Outside uses services provided by Google Maps to retrieve information about and validate the information you provided about a location.

In order to help you help us with finding locations, use the following steps to get the most accurate information about a location.

First, open Google Maps. If you don't have it installed, you can visit Google Maps from within your browser (click here to go to Google Maps - opens in new tab).

When opened, you should see a screen like this:


From here, you can do one of two things to find the details of the location you want to add:

  1. Move around the map and tap on pins to reveal details
  2. Use the search bar

  1. Zoom further into the map to give you more margin of error when tapping on the pins
  2. Tap and drag to move the focus to different places on the map.
  3. Tap on a pin to view more information about the location.
  4. Take note of the name of the location (the first words that appear at the top of the pop-up), as this is what will be needed for the "Business Name" line in app.
  5. Scroll down and copy the address, as this will be useful for filling in the rest of the information.
  6. Return to Get Outside and use the information you have found to fill in the location lookup forum

Search Bar

Click here to see details

Back to list Back to top

  1. Move the map focus to the general place where the location you want to add is
  2. Tap the search bar at the top of the screen
  3. Use one of the following methods to find the location you want to add
    1. Add the business name, followed by the town/city it's in
    2. Enter the address or post code that you want to search
    3. Use the general business name, without the city after the name.
  4. Take note of the 'title', which is the business name
  5. Scroll down and copy the address, as this will be useful for filling in the rest of the information.
  6. Return to Get Outside and use the information you have found to fill in the location lookup forum
